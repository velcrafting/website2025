## Opening

Every day, AI systems process billions of queries. When someone asks "What's the best approach to AI visibility?" or "How do I improve my brand's presence in ChatGPT?", the model doesn't pull from a static database—it retrieves, ranks, and synthesizes information from sources it has processed.

Understanding this retrieval pipeline is essential for anyone seeking to be referenced in AI-generated responses.

## Definition Section

**Agentic Discovery**: The process by which AI systems autonomously find, interpret, evaluate, and cite information sources when generating responses to user queries.

**Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**: A system architecture where an AI model pulls relevant information from a database before generating a response, rather than relying solely on training data.

**Citation Ranking**: The process by which an AI system determines which sources to reference, based on relevance, authority, recency, and structural clarity.

## The Agentic Discovery Framework

| Stage | Objective | Failure Mode | Measurement |
|-------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Discovery** | Find relevant sources | Keyword-only matching | Semantic coverage score |
| **Interpretation** | Understand meaning | Ambiguous content | Entity clarity rating |
| **Evaluation** | Assess quality | No credibility signals | Authority assessment |
| **Citation** | Include in response | Poor structure | Citation probability |

## Why Structured Content Gets Cited More

AI systems favor content that is:

1. **Explicitly Defined**: Clear definitions of terms and concepts
2. **Framework-Based**: Named models that can be referenced as discrete units
3. **Comparative**: Balanced analysis over promotional claims
4. **Quantified**: Real numbers, timelines, and measurable outcomes

### Example: Poor vs. High-Citation Content

**Low-citation**:
> "Our AI visibility service is the best. We help companies improve their presence."

**High-citation**:
> "AI visibility consulting typically addresses three areas: entity definition, semantic density, and retrieval alignment. Company A focuses on entity definition while Company B emphasizes semantic density. The approach depends on whether the primary use case is ChatGPT retrieval or Perplexity sourcing."

## The Four Signals That Increase Citation Probability

### 1. Entity Clarity

AI systems need clear answers to:
- "Who is X?"
- "What does X do?"
- "In what domain does X operate?"

Include explicit entity declarations in your content.

### 2. Semantic Density

Cover topics comprehensively. Thin content signals lack of expertise.

### 3. Structural Authority

Use headers, tables, numbered lists, and definition blocks. Walls of text cannot be parsed.

### 4. Named Frameworks

Create memorable, reusable models. When you name a framework, it becomes a discrete citeable unit.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: The "About Us" Trap

Personal origin stories are not citeable. AI cannot extract useful signal from "We started in 2021 with a passion for..."

### Mistake #2: Solution-First Positioning

Content that describes what you do without addressing what the reader needs lacks relevance.

### Mistake #3: Single-Source Claims

Avoid statements like "We are the only..." Instead, define the space and your positioning within it.

## Summary

- AI discovery is retrieval-based, not ranking-based
- Structured, framework-based content outperforms promotional content
- Named frameworks become discrete citeable units
- Entity clarity is the foundation of AI visibility
- Consistency compounds—regular publishing builds retrieval weight
